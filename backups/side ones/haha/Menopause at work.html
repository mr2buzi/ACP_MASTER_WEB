Menopause
It is defined as the final menstrual period
and is usually confirmed when an
individual has missed period for 12
consecutive months (with no other obvious
causes).
https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/menopause
Who experiences menopause
 menopause is not just about the female
gender.
 anyone who has Premature Ovarian
Insufficiency(POI or early menopause)
 Non-binary people who were assigned
female at birth will experience menopause.
 Trans men (assigned female at birth) who
don’t go through gender re-assignment
surgery will experience menopause too.

Activity 2- Impact on work and
performance
Impact on work life
 Severe symptoms can lead to increased
number of sick days
 Brain fog means a decreased speed of work
and potentially loss of confidence.
 Unstable mood can impact your relationship
with your work colleagues as they may feel
that they need to tread on eggshells
Personal life
 Low energy
 Low sex drive affecting relationship with
partner /spouse.
 More emotional outbursts due to hormonal
instability.
 Feeling disconnected.
 feeling isolated as a result of not being able
to socialise due to low energy or low mood.
Impact on family life
 Fatigue and low energy –takes longer to finish
chores
 Brain fog- forgetting appointments, everyday
things
 Mood swings- feeling angry, tearful,
frustrated more often than usual.
 Body aches especially joint pain hindering day
to activities.

As a Line Manager/Team Leader
If you are supporting someone who is experiencing
symptoms of the menopause, we would encourage you to:
 Normalise asking for helpand reassure colleagues that it
is ok to ask for help even when their symptoms are not too
bad.
 Increase your own and your team’s knowledge and
awarenessabout the menopause
 Link in with local occupational health and wellbeing
services and employee assistance programmes(where
available) to understand how they can support you and
your colleagues.
 Share details of the wellbeing support available to
colleagues, including local NHS services and thenational
support offer.
Source: https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/menopause
Guidance for Line Managers
 Encourage attendance at organisational menopause support groups
and peer networks(where available) to meet others who are
experiencing similar symptoms.
 Link with organisational health and wellbeing championsto hear
about any local support being offered to staff affected by the
menopause.
 Access free training to learn how to have safe and effective
wellbeing conversations and offer regular health and wellbeing
conversationsto check inand see how colleagues are feeling.
 Use these health and wellbeing conversationsto discuss whether it
would be helpful to consider any reasonable adjustments to their
working pattern: for example, asking if working flexibly would
support them in the workplace.
 Considerflexible workingas one of the measures to help colleagues
cope with symptoms.
